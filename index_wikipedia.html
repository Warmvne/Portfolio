Marwane Boubekeur is a French data scientist and sport scientist based in Paris, specialising in machine learning pipelines, computer vision, and performance analytics for elite sport. He holds a Master of Engineering in Data & Artificial Intelligence from ECE Paris, preceded by preparatory classes (CPGE MPSI/MP) and an exchange programme in London.

Boubekeur is primarily known for his interdisciplinary work at the intersection of sports science and data engineering, combining traditional biomechanical methodology with modern deep learning techniques. His research focuses on translating raw performance data into actionable decisions for coaching staff and sports organisations — summarised in his personal maxim: "Turning raw data into performance decisions."

As of 2026, he is open to roles in sport science, data science, and research collaborations in performance analytics. He maintains an active open-source presence under the GitHub handle Warmvne.

Education [edit]

Boubekeur completed the Classes Préparatoires aux Grandes Écoles (CPGE) in the MPSI/MP track, a competitive two-year preparatory programme in mathematics and physics that serves as a gateway to France's elite engineering schools. He subsequently enrolled at ECE Paris, where he obtained a Master of Engineering (M.Eng.) in Data & Artificial Intelligence.

During his studies he undertook an exchange programme in London, gaining international exposure to the British academic tradition and sport science ecosystem — particularly relevant to his later focus on elite performance analytics.

NBA load management and injury prediction [edit]

Main article: NBA-Project (GitHub)

Boubekeur's most cited work is an end-to-end machine learning pipeline for predicting individual NBA player injury risk from historical workload and advanced performance data. The system uses a temporal train/test split to respect chronological data ordering, preventing data leakage. Over 18 engineered features were derived from raw game logs, including acute-to-chronic workload ratios (ACWR), rolling averages, and minute-per-game trajectories.

The best-performing model — a Gradient Boosting classifier — achieved an Area Under the ROC Curve (AUC) of 0.76. Predictions are binned into three actionable tiers:

  • High risk (score ≥ 0.55): recommended rest
  • Medium risk (0.35–0.55): enhanced monitoring
  • Low risk (< 0.35): cleared to play

SHAP (SHapley Additive exPlanations) values were used to ensure model interpretability, allowing coaching staff to understand which factors contributed to a given player's risk classification. The project is reproducible via a public Google Colab notebook.

Stack: Python XGBoost SHAP Temporal CV pandas scikit-learn

Football computer vision analytics [edit]

Main article: Football_ComputerVision (GitHub)

This project constitutes a full computer vision pipeline applied to association football. Player and ball detection is performed with YOLOv8, while multi-object tracking across frames uses the ByteTrack algorithm. A bird's-eye-view projection is achieved through homography estimation, mapping pitch coordinates from broadcast camera angle to a top-down tactical view.

Voronoi diagrams are computed on the projected player positions to quantify territorial space control for each team. Additionally, the pipeline includes automated formation detection and action recognition via the R3D-18 3D convolutional network architecture. Biomechanical shooting analysis is carried out with MediaPipe pose estimation, examining joint angles and kinetic chain sequencing.

Stack: YOLOv8 ByteTrack OpenCV MediaPipe R3D-18 Homography

Basketball computer vision analytics [edit]

Main article: Basketball_ComputerVision (GitHub)

The basketball CV system adapts and extends the football pipeline to NBA analysis. Shooting mechanics are evaluated via MediaPipe using the BEEF principle (Balance, Eyes, Elbow, Follow-through), with automated measurement of elbow angle and knee valgus during the shooting motion.

Offensive spacing is quantified through a composite score derived from Voronoi area and convex hull computations on player positions. Ball movement patterns are modelled as directed graphs using NetworkX, enabling the identification of passing networks and playmaking tendencies. Seven distinct action classes (pick-and-roll, isolation, transition, etc.) are recognised using an R3D-18 classifier trained on curated video clips.

Stack: YOLOv8 MediaPipe R3D-18 NetworkX scipy nba_api

Other projects [edit]

Sport Scientist Curriculum is an open-source, self-directed learning resource comprising a six-month programme across statistics, machine learning theory, Python, R, football analytics (including StatsBomb data and expected goals modelling), and basketball analytics (NBA advanced metrics, tracking data). The curriculum consists of 37 Jupyter notebooks, each with exercises and worked solutions.

BrainBridge is a Chrome extension that intercepts API requests to AI services and launches brief cognitive mini-games during the resultant wait period, reducing idle time and improving perceived responsiveness of AI-powered tools.

Acoustic Person Localization was developed during a hackathon. It applies signal processing and deep learning to indoor person localization using Room Impulse Responses (RIRs) from microphone arrays.

Technical expertise [edit]

Boubekeur's skill set spans the full data-to-decision stack, from raw data ingestion and warehousing to deployed inference pipelines and interactive visualisations.

Machine learning and statistical modelling

Core methods include gradient boosting (XGBoost), random forests, temporal cross-validation, SHAP interpretability, survival analysis (Cox Proportional Hazards), unsupervised clustering (K-Means, DBSCAN), and dimensionality reduction (PCA, UMAP).

Computer vision

YOLOv8 for object detection and tracking, ByteTrack for multi-object tracking, MediaPipe for pose estimation, homography and perspective transforms, R3D-18 for 3D action recognition, and Farneback optical flow.

Sports analytics

Load management and ACWR modelling, expected goals (xG) model development with StatsBomb data, NBA advanced metrics (BPM, WS, RAPTOR), GPS and wearable data analysis, player profiling and similarity tools, tactical analysis through Voronoi decomposition and formation detection.
